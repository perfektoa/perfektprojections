================================================================================
TGS PROJECTIONS - PLAYER VALUATION SYSTEMS
================================================================================

This document explains the two player valuation systems used in the TGS
Projections web app: Future Value (FV) and Draft FV. They answer different
questions and are designed to work alongside each other.


================================================================================
1. FUTURE VALUE (FV) — "What is this player's total career value?"
================================================================================

PURPOSE:
  FV measures the total cumulative WAA (Wins Above Average) a player is
  projected to produce from now through the end of their career. It answers
  the trade/roster question: "How much total value will I get from this guy?"

  Displayed as both a raw dollar figure (Future$) and a 20-80 scouting scale (FV).

DATA INPUTS:
  - Current WAA: Best of Max WAA wtd, Max WAA vR, WAA wtd, WAA wtd RP
  - Potential WAA: Best of MAX WAA P, WAP, WAP RP (only exists for ages ~16-23)
  - Player age

HOW IT WORKS:

  Step 1 — Determine Current vs Potential
    The model reads the player's best current WAA across all positions/splits,
    then checks if potential ratings exist. Players age 24+ typically have no
    potential data — they are "established" and their current WAA IS their value.
    Younger players have a "gap" between current and potential that represents
    their development upside.

  Step 2 — Development S-Curve (Gap Factor)
    For prospects, the model uses a logistic S-curve to estimate how much of
    the current-to-potential gap they'll close by each age:
      - Age 16: ~5% developed (barely started)
      - Age 20: ~50% developed (inflection point)
      - Age 25: ~95% developed (maturity — OOTP stops development here)
    The steepness parameter (0.6) controls how sharp the S-curve is.

  Step 3 — Risk Factor
    Not every prospect reaches their potential. The risk factor (0.80 to 0.95)
    discounts the expected peak based on two things:
      a) Development progress (60% weight): Older = more certain. A 22-year-old
         near maturity is safer than a 16-year-old.
      b) Gap magnitude (40% weight): Smaller gaps are safer. Closing a 2 WAA
         gap is much more likely than closing a 13 WAA gap.
    Established players get the maximum risk credit (0.95 — no development risk).

  Step 4 — Expected Peak WAA
    For prospects: currentWAA + gap x 0.95 (max dev) x riskFactor
    For established: just their current WAA (they are what they are)

  Step 5 — Year-by-Year Projection
    Starting from the player's current age through age 34:
      - Before age 25: WAA follows the development S-curve toward peak
      - At age 25: Peak (maturity reached)
      - Ages 25-30: 6% annual decline
      - Ages 30+: 12% annual decline (the "cliff")
    Each future year is also discounted by 3% annually for time value
    (a WAA produced 8 years from now is worth less than one produced today).

  Step 6 — Cumulative Future Value
    PROSPECTS: Only count WAA from age 25 onward (skip development years).
    A 17-year-old producing -8 WAA in Rookie ball shouldn't be penalized for
    that — what matters is what they'll produce once they mature.

    ESTABLISHED PLAYERS: Sum all projected years from current age forward.
    They're already producing; every year counts.

  Step 7 — 20-80 Scale Conversion
    The raw cumulative WAA is mapped to the scouting scale:
      Raw -15 -> FV 20 (no value)
      Raw   0 -> FV 40 (replacement level)
      Raw   5 -> FV 50 (fringe regular)
      Raw  14 -> FV 60 (solid regular)
      Raw  28 -> FV 70 (franchise player)
      Raw  45 -> FV 80 (elite/generational)

COLUMNS DISPLAYED:
  FV (20-80)      The scouting scale grade
  Future$         The raw cumulative projected WAA
  Current WAA     Best current WAA across all positions
  Potential WAA   Best potential WAA (or current if no potential data)
  Peak WAA        Highest single-year projected WAA
  % to Peak       How close current WAA is to potential (0-100%)
  Yrs to Peak     Years until maturity age (25)

KEY BEHAVIORS:
  - Young prospects with high potential but bad current WAA can still have
    high FV because the model projects forward past the development years.
  - Older players (28+) have declining FV because the aging curve eats into
    their remaining productive years.
  - FV deliberately penalizes youth through time discounting — a prospect
    who won't produce for 7 years has lower present value than a player
    producing right now.
  - This makes FV excellent for TRADE decisions (total remaining value)
    but less useful for DRAFT decisions (who has the most upside).


================================================================================
2. DRAFT FV — "How good is this prospect relative to his age?"
================================================================================

PURPOSE:
  Draft FV was built specifically for the amateur draft. It answers two
  questions that matter when scouting draft-eligible players:
    1. Can this player compete against others his age RIGHT NOW?
    2. How high is his ceiling by maturity?

  Unlike FV (which penalizes youth through cumulative projections and time
  discounting), Draft FV rewards players who are already performing well
  relative to their age group, and weights ceiling potential heavily.

FORMULA:
  Draft FV Raw = (Age Percentile x 0.35 + Ceiling Score x 0.65)
                  x Durability Modifier x Work Ethic Modifier

  Then the raw score (0-100) is converted to a 20-80 scouting scale.

DATA INPUTS:
  - Hitters: wOBA wtd (current performance), MAX WAA P (ceiling)
  - Pitchers: WAA wtd (current performance), WAP (ceiling)
  - Prone column: injury durability tier
  - WE column: work ethic rating
  - INT column: intelligence (flag only, does not affect the number)
  - Full league population (all hitters or all pitchers, not just the draft pool)

COMPONENT BREAKDOWN:

  Age Percentile (35% of score):
    Compares the player's current performance metric against ALL players in the
    full league at the same integer age — not just the draft pool.

    For hitters, the metric is wOBA wtd (weighted on-base average, which
    measures pure hitting ability). For pitchers, it's WAA wtd (overall
    pitcher value weighted by splits).

    Using the full league population (~500 players per age bucket) gives
    statistical stability, versus the draft pool alone (~30-130 players)
    which would be noisy.

    A player who ranks in the 90th percentile of his age group for wOBA
    is clearly competing well and developing on track.

  Ceiling Score (65% of score):
    Measures the player's maximum potential, normalized to a 0-100 scale.

    For hitters: MAX WAA P (potential WAA at best position)
      Floor: -3.0 WAA -> score 0
      Cap:    3.0 WAA -> score 100

    For pitchers: WAP (potential WAA for pitchers)
      Floor: -3.0 WAA -> score 0
      Cap:    1.5 WAA -> score 100 (pitchers have lower WAA ceilings)

    For mature players (age 25+) who have no potential ratings, the ceiling
    falls back to their current best WAA. If a player is already producing
    3.3 WAA at age 25, that IS their ceiling — and it's elite.

  Durability Modifier:
    Multiplies the entire score based on the Prone column:
      Wrecked:   0.00 (undraftable — Draft FV forced to 20)
      Fragile:   0.75 (25% penalty — will miss time, develop slower)
      Normal:    0.95 (5% penalty — typical injury risk)
      Durable:   1.00 (no penalty)
      Iron Man:  1.00 (no penalty, no bonus)

    Fragile players develop ~25% slower because injuries compound — more
    time missed means less development, and injuries breed more injuries.

  Work Ethic Modifier:
    High work ethic (WE = "H") gives a 1.05x multiplier (+5% boost).
    Normal and Low work ethic have no effect (1.0x).
    Work ethic accelerates development — high WE players close their
    development gap faster.

  Intelligence (INT):
    NOT factored into the number. Displayed as a flag column (Y/-).
    High INT increases the chance of a positive TCR (talent change report),
    making it a useful tiebreaker in middle and late rounds. A high INT
    prospect who already competes at his age level has a chance to "pop off"
    from a lucky development boost.

20-80 SCALE CONVERSION:
  Raw   0 -> Draft FV 20
  Raw  15 -> Draft FV 30
  Raw  30 -> Draft FV 35
  Raw  40 -> Draft FV 40
  Raw  50 -> Draft FV 45
  Raw  60 -> Draft FV 50
  Raw  70 -> Draft FV 55
  Raw  78 -> Draft FV 60
  Raw  85 -> Draft FV 65
  Raw  92 -> Draft FV 70
  Raw 100 -> Draft FV 80

  The scale is deliberately compressed at the top — only the very best
  prospects (elite age percentile + elite ceiling + durable + high WE)
  can reach 70+.

COLUMNS DISPLAYED:
  Draft FV (20-80)  The scouting scale grade
  Draft Raw         The 0-100 composite score before scale conversion
  Age Pctl          Percentile rank within age group (0-100%)
  Ceiling           Raw ceiling metric value (MAX WAA P or WAP)
  Durability        Prone tier (Wrecked/Fragile/Normal/Durable/Iron Man)
  INT               High intelligence flag (Y/-)

KEY BEHAVIORS:
  - A young player (e.g., age 18) with strong wOBA relative to other
    18-year-olds AND high ceiling potential will score very well.
  - A mature player (age 25+) with no potential data uses current WAA
    as ceiling. If they're producing 3+ WAA, that maxes out the ceiling
    score and they get rated appropriately.
  - Wrecked players are instantly flagged as undraftable (FV 20).
  - Fragile players take a significant 25% hit — a player who would be
    a 60 Draft FV as Durable becomes roughly a 50 as Fragile.
  - The 65/35 ceiling-to-current split means ceiling matters most, but
    a player still needs to show they can compete NOW to get a high grade.

3. HOW THEY WORK TOGETHER
================================================================================

  FV (Future Value):
    Best for: Trade decisions, roster construction, contract valuation
    Answers: "How much total WAA will this player produce over their career?"
    Favors: Established players in their productive years (25-30)
    Penalizes: Youth (time discount), low current production

  Draft FV:
    Best for: Amateur draft decisions, prospect ranking
    Answers: "How good is this player relative to his age, and how high can he go?"
    Favors: Players who compete above their age group with high ceilings
    Penalizes: Injury-prone players, low ceilings, poor age-relative performance

  A player can have HIGH Draft FV but LOW FV:
    An 18-year-old with 90th percentile wOBA for his age and a 2.5 WAA ceiling
    would rank well on Draft FV, but the cumulative career projection (FV)
    discounts him heavily because peak production is 7 years away.

  A player can have LOW Draft FV but HIGH FV:
    A 26-year-old producing 2.0 WAA will have poor Draft FV (not draft-eligible,
    and age percentile competes against other 26-year-olds) but solid FV because
    he's producing right now with years of value left.

  Use both together to see the full picture:
    - Draft Board: Sort by Draft FV for your pick order
    - Team sheets: Look at FV/Future$ for trade and roster decisions
    - Both are visible on the Hitters and Pitchers pages


================================================================================
4. DRAFT BOARD DATA PIPELINE
================================================================================

  The extract_data.py script handles the data:
    1. Reads "The Sheet Hitters - Draft.xlsx" / "The Sheet Pitchers - Draft.xlsx"
    2. From the "Player List" sheet: identifies players marked Manual='DRAFT'
       with an actual name — these are the draft-eligible amateur pool
    3. From the "Drafted" sheet: reads the StatsPlus CSV paste to get IDs
       of players already picked
    4. Filters the main "Hitters"/"Pitchers" sheet to only include players
       whose ID is draft-eligible AND not yet drafted
    5. Outputs the filtered data as hitters_draft.json / pitchers_draft.json

  To update after new draft picks:
    1. Paste the updated draft API data from StatsPlus into the "Drafted" tab
    2. Run "Update Data.bat" (which calls python extract_data.py)
    3. Refresh the browser — the draft board will exclude newly-drafted players


================================================================================
5. TUNABLE PARAMETERS
================================================================================

  FV parameters (in futureValue.js):
    MATURITY_AGE: 25        Age when development stops
    GAP_MAX: 0.95           Max fraction of potential gap reached
    GAP_STEEPNESS: 0.6      S-curve steepness
    RISK_FLOOR: 0.80        Minimum risk credit
    RISK_CEILING: 0.95      Maximum risk credit
    PEAK_END: 25            End of peak performance
    DECLINE_RATE: 0.06      Annual decline after peak (6%)
    CLIFF_AGE: 30           When decline accelerates
    CLIFF_RATE: 0.12        Annual decline after cliff (12%)
    DISCOUNT_RATE: 0.03     Annual time discount (3%)
    MAX_CAREER_AGE: 34      Stop projecting beyond this age

  Draft FV parameters (in draftFV.js):
    CURRENT_WEIGHT: 0.35       Age percentile weight
    CEILING_WEIGHT: 0.65       Ceiling score weight
    HITTER_CEILING_FLOOR: -3.0 Hitter ceiling min (maps to 0)
    HITTER_CEILING_CAP: 3.0    Hitter ceiling max (maps to 100)
    PITCHER_CEILING_FLOOR: -3.0 Pitcher ceiling min
    PITCHER_CEILING_CAP: 1.5    Pitcher ceiling max

  All parameters can be adjusted in the source files. The Dev Analysis page
  in the web app also allows tuning FV parameters interactively.
